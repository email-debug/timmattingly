## Content Calendar — Month 1

### Week 1: The Origin Story
**Post:** "6 months ago I decided to build a travel planning app. I'm not a developer..."
- Short post (not article), hook above the fold
- End with: "Has anyone else tried building real software with AI? What broke first?"

### Week 2: The Failure Stories
**Post:** "The AI coding platform that rewrote my entire app when I asked it what 2+2 was."
- Relatable to anyone who's tried AI coding tools
- Shows you've done the homework, not just theory

### Week 3: The System
**Post:** "I built a project manager for AI agents. Here's why."
- Introduce the orchestration concept without jargon
- 3-4 concrete examples of what it does

### Week 4: The Article
**Article:** "I'm Not a Developer. I Shipped a Production SaaS."
- Full story with teaser post
- This is the flagship piece

## Content Pillars (Ongoing)

1. **Builder stories** (40%) — What I built, what broke, what I learned
2. **AI agent insights** (30%) — Patterns for making AI reliable, failure modes
3. **Career evolution** (20%) — The changing role of technical leadership
4. **Travel / STP** (10%) — Product updates, user stories

## Rules
- Post 2-3x per week maximum (quality over quantity)
- Always respond to comments within 24 hours
- Never be salesy — share learning, invite discussion
- Use "I" not "we" — personal brand, not corporate
- Screenshots and demos beat text every time

## Engagement Strategy
- Comment thoughtfully on 5 posts/day from network (10 min)
- Share other people's AI coding experiences with your take
- DM people who engage deeply — warm relationship building
- Join relevant LinkedIn groups (AI, startup CTO, indie hackers)
